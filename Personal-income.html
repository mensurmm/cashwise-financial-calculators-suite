Personal Income Tax Ethiopia

Personal Income Tax in Ethiopia Earning income in Ethiopia? Whether you receive a salary, work as a freelancer, or run your own business, understanding Personal Income Tax (PIT) is essential. Ethiopia applies a progressive tax system, with rates ranging from 0% to 35%, so higher earnings are taxed at higher rates. Taxable income includes wages, bonuses, allowances, rental income, and self-employment earnings. For most employees, tax is automatically deducted by the employer, making compliance simple. Self-employed individuals, however, must file an annual return with the Ethiopian Revenue and Customs Authority (ERCA). Residents are taxed on worldwide income, while non-residents are only taxed on income from Ethiopian sources. Certain deductions, such as pension contributions, can help reduce your taxable income.

Tax Brackets (Monthly Income)

  • 0 – 600 ETB → 0% (Exempt)
  • 601 – 1650 ETB → 10% on excess over 600
  • 1651 – 3200 ETB → 15% on excess over 1650 + fixed 105 ETB
  • 3201 – 5250 ETB → 20% on excess over 3200 + fixed 367.5 ETB
  • 5251 – 7800 ETB → 25% on excess over 5250 + fixed 867.5 ETB
  • 7801 – 10900 ETB → 30% on excess over 7800 + fixed 1505 ETB
  • Above 10900 ETB → 35% on excess over 10900 + fixed 2405 ETB
for more info Visit Ministry of Revenue Ethiopia
