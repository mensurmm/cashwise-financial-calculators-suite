Compound Interest

Compound interest is interest calculated on the principal + previously earned interest. Formula (basic): A = P(1 + r)ᵗ. It’s used in long-term savings, investments, mortgages, and most bank loans, because the amount grows faster over time.

Ethiopia

Banks mainly use compound interest for savings accounts, fixed deposits, and many loan products, with rates similar to simple interest products (e.g., savings around 8%, lending around 14%+), but the total paid or earned becomes more due to compounding.
