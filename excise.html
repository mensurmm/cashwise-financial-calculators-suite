Excise Tax Ethiopia

Excise tax in Ethiopia is a special tax imposed on selected goods and services that are considered either luxury items, harmful to health or the environment, or goods whose consumption the government wants to regulate. The tax applies to both locally manufactured products and imported goods. It is governed by Excise Tax Proclamation No. 1186/2020 and its amendment Proclamation No. 1287/2023, which updated tax rates and expanded or reduced the list of excisable items.
The main goal of excise tax is not just revenue generation but also discouraging the use of harmful or non-essential products, such as alcohol, tobacco, old or highly polluting vehicles, and single-use plastics. At the same time, the government uses excise tax to encourage healthier consumption patterns and protect local industries.

The tax base differs depending on the origin of the product
  • For imported goods, excise is calculated on the customs value plus customs duty.
  • For locally produced goods, it is calculated on the ex-factory price before VAT.

Major categories under excise tax include:

  • Alcoholic drinks, beer, wine, and spirits.
  • Tobacco and cigarettes, which have both percentage and per-kilogram taxes.
  • Sugar-based and fatty foods, sweets, chocolates, and certain oils.
  • Vehicles, especially used cars or large-engine cars, which attract high excise tax to discourage environmental impact and reduce foreign currency outflow.
  • Plastic products, such as plastic bags and bottles.
  • Luxury goods like cosmetics, artificial hair, carpets, and selected textiles.
