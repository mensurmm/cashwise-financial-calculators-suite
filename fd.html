Fixed Deposit (FD)

A fixed deposit is a type of bank account where you lock your money for a fixed period (e.g., 3 months, 6 months, 1 year) in exchange for a higher interest rate than a normal savings account. You cannot freely withdraw the money before the period ends. The interest rate is fixed from the start (it does not change).

Ethiopia

Most banks offer around 7–8% per year on deposit-type accounts (FD-equivalent rates).
