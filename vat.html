VAT in Ethiopia

VAT (Value-Added Tax) in Ethiopia is a consumption tax charged on most goods and services, including products sold locally or imported. It is not only for services almost all commercial goods are VAT-taxable unless specifically exempt. The standard VAT rate is 15%, added at the point of sale. Essential items such as staple cereals and pulses (like teff and wheat), their flours, basic foods like injera, bread, and milk, agricultural inputs (fertilizers, seeds, pesticides), certain medical items, health services, education, transport services, and some printed materials are exempt from VAT, meaning no tax is added. Exports and international transport services are zero-rated (0% VAT). Most non-essential goods—electronics, clothing, processed products, general services, and imports—are fully taxed at 15%. Recent updates by the Ministry of Finance revised the exemption list, but the core essential goods and basic services remain tax-free. Overall, VAT in Ethiopia applies broadly to both products and services, with specific exceptions to help keep essential living costs affordable.
